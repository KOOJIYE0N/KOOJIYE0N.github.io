Research focus

Robotic intelligence for physical, spatial, and mission-critical tasks.

My work centers on embodied AI systems that make robot arms more capable in complex environments: reusing visual model representations for spatial reasoning, generating learning data for docking assistance, and adapting action policies to moving targets.

Across these projects, the through-line is practical autonomy: policies that can see, reason, and act reliably on real manipulators and aerospace-oriented test scenarios.

01

Spatial VLA Models

Lightweight vision-language-action policies that preserve scene layout and 3D context for manipulation.

02

Learning from Demonstration

Imitation learning pipelines for robot-arm control, docking assistance, and task-oriented data generation.

03

Robotics for Aerospace

Manipulation methods for space docking support and aerial recovery systems with moving objects.

ICRA 2026 / arXiv 2025-2026

RetoVLA: Reusing Register Tokens for Spatial Reasoning in Vision-Language-Action Models

RetoVLA repurposes register tokens from vision transformers as global spatial context for robot action generation. The method injects these tokens into the action expert, improving spatial reasoning while keeping the model lightweight.

  • Focus: efficient VLA architectures, spatial context injection, 7-DOF manipulation.
  • Reported result: 17.1 percentage-point improvement in average real-world success rates over baseline.

DAUS 2026 2026

Kalman-Conditioned Action Chunking Transformers for Dynamic Object Grasping Toward Aerial Recovery Systems

A dynamic grasping study that conditions Action Chunking Transformers with state estimation cues, aiming to improve robot-arm interaction with moving objects in aerial recovery scenarios.

  • Focus: dynamic object grasping, Kalman-conditioned policies, aerial recovery systems.
  • Venue signal: listed in the Drones and Unmanned Systems 2026 conference program.

BB-VLA: Brain-Body 프레임워크로 사람의 사고를 모방하는 지능형 매니퓰레이터 팔레타이징

BB-VLA frames robot palletizing as a brain-body system, separating higher-level reasoning from embodied manipulation to imitate human-like task planning and action.
